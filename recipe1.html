BISI BELLA BATH

Bisi bella bath is a classic dish from Karnataka,India. Bisi bella Bath is a traditional South Indian dish made with rice,lentils,mixed vegetables, tamarind and a unique blend of spices. It's flavorful,hearty and often enjoyed with a dollop of ghee on top.

INGREDIENTS:

  • Rice:Short grain rice is preferred.
  • Lentils: Toor dal (split pigeon peas) is commonly used.
  • Vegetables: A mix of carrots, peas, beans, and bell peppers.
  • Tamarind: For the tangy flavor.
  • Jaggery: Adds a hint of sweetness.
  • Bisi Bele Bath Powder: A special spice blend that includes coriander seeds, cumin, dry red chilies, fenugreak seeds, cinnamon, cloves, cardamon, and assafoetida.

PROCEDURE:

  1. Cook Rice and Lentils: Rinse the rice and toor dal separately. Cook them until the dal turns mushy.
  2. Prepare Vegetables: Chop the vegetables and cook them until tender.
  3. Make Bisi Bele Bath Powder: Roast the spices and grind them into a fine powder. You can also use a store-bought powder.
  4. Combine and Cook: Mix the cooked rice, dal, vegetables, tamarind pulp, jaggery, and the spice powder. Cook everything together until the flavors meld and the consistency is gooey.

TIPS:

For a flavorful Sambar Satham, use a mix of feeshly roasted spices for the Sambar powder and cook rice and lentils until mushy for the right consistency. Serve hot, garnished with ghee and fried cashews for an added touch of richness.
