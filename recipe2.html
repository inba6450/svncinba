PULAO

Pulao, also known as pilaf, is a fragrant rice dish cooked with spices and vegetables. Here's a basic recipe for making vegetable pulao: Pulao is a popular rice dish cooked with fragrant spices, vegetables, and sometimes meat or seafood. It's often served as a main course or as a side dish in many cuisines, particularly in South Asia and the Middle East.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1.5 cups basmati rice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamoms
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 star anise (optional)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit(adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, cauliflower)
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 3 cups water
  • Salt to taste

PROCEDURE:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice in water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pot. Add cinnamon, cloves, cardamons, bay leaf, and star anise. Saute until they become aromatic.
  3. Add the sliced onions and saute until they turn golden brown.
  4. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and fry for a minute.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables and mint leaves. Saute for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the drained rice and saite gently for another 2 minutes to coat the rice with the spices.
  7. pour in 3 cups of water and add salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and cook until the rice is done and the water has been absorbed, about 15-20 minutes.
  8. Fluff the rice gently with a fork and serve hot with raita or any cuury of your choice.
